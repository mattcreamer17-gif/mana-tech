The frame

Most of what a cohort program does is not an AI problem.

Reading applications, taking mentor calls, renting coworking desks, running a demo day. That is traditional ops with good people. Roughly ten percent of it is genuine AI work: founder-interview summarization, rubric extraction, mentor-to-founder matching across twenty-two countries of context, cohort-aware email drafting, localization of curriculum into Portuguese and Spanish without losing edge.

The trap is spending on the ninety percent where a shared doc would do. The edge is scoping the ten percent tightly and building it once so the next cohort runs without the MD in the loop.

The 10%
  • Rubric extraction from applications
  • Founder-interview summarization
  • Mentor-to-founder matching at cohort scale
  • Curriculum localization (PT / ES) without losing edge

The 90%

The opening

Jake spent the last week reading everything public about how Mana Tech runs a cohort.

From the NPS of 85 across 255 startups, to Etienne's Nexuma interview on Climate Tech Scale-Up, to the two-cohort-a-year Scale2Miami rhythm, to the ZYSCOVICH-designed Tesla Hub at 155 South Miami. He came back with a specific hypothesis about where the machine leaks time.

  1. 01

    Application triage is manual.

    Six editions a year. Sector rubrics for climate, agri-food, fintech. A program manager is burning one to two weeks per cohort reading applications and cross-referencing against mentor availability. A scoped orchestration layer compresses that to under two days and creates an auditable scoring trail that trade-body sponsors like ProChile and Uruguay XXI will actually value.

  2. 02

    Mentor matching does not scale with the team.

    150+ mentors, 330+ startups supported. Matching quality is a point of pride, and is almost certainly living in the MD's head. An instrumented matching layer with a folder structure per cohort (program identity, sector thesis, mentor profiles, founder profiles, session notes) hands the next program hire a working tool on day one.

  3. 03

    Tesla Hub leasing is under-instrumented against cohort alumni.

    137,000 sq ft to fill, with Mana Tech's cohort graduates and their international networks as the primary demand source. There is room for a CRM-level view that connects cohort alumni to leasing interest to Flagler tenant conversion.

The paper behind it

Interpretable Context Methodology.

A cohort program reads as a near-textbook ICM use case. L0 is program identity (soft-landing, international, sector-specific). L1 is reusable methodology (rubrics, mentor protocols). L2 is stage contracts (what "admitted" or "demo-day-ready" means). L3 is the current-cycle factory (founder profiles, mentor profiles, session notes). L4 is the working artifacts (decks, one-pagers, investor briefs).

Mana Tech is already running this structure informally. Making it explicit and machine-addressable is the work.
